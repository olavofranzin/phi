# Platform Specs and Segment Adjustments

Reference material for the campaign-brief-generator skill. Read this file when generating the deliverables table and tailoring the brief to the brand's segment.

## Platform-Specific Deliverable Guidance

Use these notes when populating the Deliverables table and platform-specific instructions in the brief.

### TikTok

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical |
| Optimal duration | 15-60 seconds |
| Sound | Sound-on environment — trending audio encouraged |
| Aesthetic | Raw, native-feeling outperforms polished production |
| Product integration | Casual, mid-content placement outperforms scripted intros |
| Captions | Text overlays critical for discoverability; platform auto-generates captions |
| Posting notes | Post natively (not cross-posted from Reels); use relevant hashtags |

### Instagram Reels

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical |
| Optimal duration | 15-90 seconds (15-45 for reach, 60-90 for saves and shares) |
| Sound | Sound-off common — caption and text overlays carry the message |
| Aesthetic | Slightly more polished than TikTok; visual-first |
| Product integration | Works well in tutorials, routines, and "favorites" formats |
| Captions | Caption matters — add context and CTA in the caption |
| Posting notes | Post to feed (not just Stories); use Reels-specific hashtags |

### Instagram Stories

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical |
| Optimal duration | 15 seconds per frame; 3-5 frames typical |
| Sound | Sound-off common — text overlays essential |
| Aesthetic | Can be more casual and behind-the-scenes than feed content |
| Product integration | Swipe-up link or product sticker required if available |
| Captions | Use text overlays for viewers watching with sound off |
| Posting notes | Stories expire in 24 hours — plan capture timing accordingly |

### YouTube Long-Form (Integrations)

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 horizontal |
| Optimal duration | 60-120 second integration within a longer video |
| Sound | Sound-on environment; detailed voiceover expected |
| Aesthetic | Higher production value expected; well-lit, edited |
| Product integration | Works best as a natural segment, not a scripted ad read |
| Captions | Include link in video description; mention product name verbally |
| Posting notes | Most formal platform — creators expect detailed briefs and clear deliverable specs |

### YouTube Shorts

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical |
| Optimal duration | 15-45 seconds; tighter pacing than Reels |
| Sound | Sound-on; strong visual or statement opening |
| Aesthetic | Between TikTok rawness and Reels polish |
| Product integration | Works well in educational, comparison, or quick-tip formats |
| Captions | Optimize for searchable titles and topics |
| Posting notes | Discovery-oriented — can drive traffic to long-form YouTube content |

### Instagram Static / Carousel

| Spec | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Aspect ratio | 1:1 or 4:5 |
| Format | Single image or 2-10 slide carousel |
| Aesthetic | Polished, editorial — highest visual bar of all Instagram formats |
| Product integration | Direct and expected — audiences accept product-focused carousels |
| Captions | Long-form captions perform well; include CTA |
| Posting notes | Carousel posts drive higher saves and shares than single images; best for educational, listicle, or step-by-step concepts |

## Segment-Aware Brief Adjustments

Tailor the brief based on who is sending it and the scale of their program.

### SMB Brands (Solo Marketer, Under 50 Creators)

- Keep the brief to 1-2 pages. These creators are often nano/micro and respond better to shorter, more personal briefs.
- Lead with the product and what the creator gets — skip extensive brand backstory.
- The brief is often sent via DM or email, not as a PDF. Format for easy reading on mobile.
- Focus on 2-3 content angles maximum. Too many options overwhelm solo marketers who then cannot pick.
- Default compensation context: gifting programs and small flat fees are most common at this stage.
- Tracking context: most SMB teams track creator content manually — in spreadsheets, Google Drive folders, and email threads. The brief should be self-contained enough that the marketer does not need a separate system to manage it.

### Mid-Market Brands (Dedicated Influencer Team, 50-200 Creators)

- Full brief with all sections. This is the document the team shares internally and sends to creators.
- Include the campaign overview section for stakeholder context — internal reviewers (brand managers, legal) need to understand the campaign intent.
- Tag which angles are awareness-focused vs. conversion-focused so the team can assign the right angles to the right creator tiers.
- Include usage rights and whitelisting terms — Mid-Market teams are starting to repurpose creator content for paid.
- Include a content approval workflow: who reviews drafts, how feedback is sent, and the revision turnaround time.
- Tracking context: Mid-Market teams often use a mix of spreadsheets and platforms. If the brand uses Archive or a similar platform, note it in the Resources section so creators know content capture is automated.

### Enterprise Brands and Agencies (200+ Creators)

- Full brief plus an executive summary at the top for stakeholders who will not read the full document.
- Include detailed usage rights, exclusivity windows, and content approval workflow steps.
- Agencies: write the brief in the client brand's voice, not the agency's voice.
- Include a section on content versioning if the brand needs multiple edits (organic version, paid version, trimmed version for Stories).
- Legal review: Enterprise briefs often go through legal before distribution — include a clear section on claims restrictions and disclosure requirements that legal can approve quickly.
- Tracking context: Enterprise teams and agencies typically use dedicated platforms. Reference the brand's platform in the Resources section and note any automated content capture workflows.
